1) Executive Summary

You want a unified CRM & ShopOS that replaces your current tool stack and creates one system for lead flow, quoting, scheduling, job execution, payments, and communication. The goal is to validate it in your shop, then sell it to other shops as a subscription service.

2) Goals & Outcomes
Operate your shop
  • Centralize leads, customers, vehicles, services, tasks, scheduling, quotes, invoices, and payments
  • Reduce tool sprawl and manual follow-up
  • Improve conversion tracking and sales accountability
  • Enable role-based workflows for admin, sales, and techs
Build SaaS foundation
  • Multi-tenant architecture ready for additional shops
  • Templates and onboarding path for future customers
  • Reporting and audit logging suitable for scale
3) Requirements Confirmed
  • Single-shop launch first, SaaS and multi-shop later (product-ready from day one)
  • Payments: deposits and balance, processed through Stripe & Square
  • Communication: two-way SMS & calling, tied to customer records
  • Devices: desktop-first and tablet-friendly workflows (mobile-first not required)
  • Core modules: customers, vehicles, pipeline, scheduling, quoting, invoicing, dashboards, reporting, recommended services
  • Migration: import of customers, active leads, and upcoming appointments from existing tools
4) Technical Architecture

The prototype UI is built with React, TypeScript, Vite, Tailwind, and shadcn/ui components. The build will add a production backend, role-based access, integrations, and audit-grade logging.

Backend recommendation: Supabase (Postgres) for authentication, database, storage, Row Level Security, and APIs. This fits CRM reporting and structured relational data better than a purely document-based approach.
Integrations: Twilio (SMS & calling), Stripe, Square, and an email provider for confirmations and receipts. Monitoring and error tracking will be enabled for stability.
Note: Vendor accounts (Twilio, Stripe, Square, hosting) are owned and billed directly to the client. See Section 7.
5) Scope of Work
A) Platform foundation
  • Authentication and role-based access (Owner, Admin, Sales, Tech)
  • Multi-tenant data model (Organization, Location, Users) to support future SaaS
  • API layer, audit logs, and environment setup (dev, staging, production)
B) CRM: customers and vehicles
  • Customer records with multiple vehicles per customer
  • Service history, notes, tags, attachments
  • Recommended services (rules-based initially, AI-enhanced later if desired)
C) Sales pipeline and follow-up
  • Pipeline stages, lead sources, deal values, assignment and activity logs
  • Follow-up tasks and reminders with accountability visibility
  • Conversion reporting baseline
D) Scheduling and capacity
  • Calendar scheduling with duration presets and service categories
  • Basic capacity rules, bay and tech assignment support
  • Confirmations (email first, SMS expanded with Twilio)
E) Quote, booking, invoice workflow
  • Quote builder aligned to your package and add-on structure
  • Approval flow, convert quote to booking and invoice
  • Status tracking: Draft, Sent, Approved, Scheduled, Completed, Paid
F) Payments
  • Stripe and Square integration options
  • Deposit and balance workflows
  • Automatic status updates via webhooks and reconciliation view
G) Communication
  • Two-way SMS inbox tied to customer records (Twilio)
  • Click-to-call and call logging (Twilio)
  • Opt-out compliance and suppression handling
H) Dashboards and reporting
  • Owner dashboard: conversion rates, revenue indicators, performance
  • Sales dashboard: pipeline value, tasks, schedule and expected revenue
  • Tech dashboard: assigned jobs, daily workload (tablet-friendly)
I) Data migration
  • Import of customers, active leads, and upcoming appointments
  • Mapping rules documented and validated
